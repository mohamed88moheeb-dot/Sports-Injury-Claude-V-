# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Injured athletes and active people managing their own sports injury rehab. They open the app in pain or discomfort, want a clear next step, and return daily to follow their structured plan. Context: at home, post-workout, or between clinic visits. Primary job: complete today's session, track progress, and feel in control of their recovery.

## Product Purpose

InjuryGuide is an AI-assessed, criteria-based recovery platform that replaces guesswork with a personalised day-by-day rehab plan. It exists because injured athletes don't know what to do next and generic advice doesn't account for their body, sport, or stage. Success = pain resolved, injury understood, daily habit built — all three after 4 weeks.

## Brand Personality

Clinically precise, futuristically confident, warmly human. Like a world-class physio who also happens to be a brilliant AI — authoritative without being cold, premium without being sterile. Three words: **precise, empowering, elevated**.

## Anti-references

- Generic NHS / hospital portals — sterile bureaucratic blue-on-white forms, no personality
- Wellness / meditation apps (Calm, Headspace) — soft pastels, organic shapes, slow ambient vibes
- These two failure modes bracket the design space: don't feel like a medical form, don't feel like a spa app

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — every screen has one primary action; complexity lives one tap deeper
2. **Progress is the product** — completion, streaks, and forward momentum are the emotional core
3. **Earned confidence** — the AI's authority must be felt through precision (exact reps, specific cues, criteria-based gates), not asserted through copy
4. **Glass is purposeful, not decorative** — liquid glass surfaces carry hierarchy and depth; they're not applied by default
5. **Motion serves recovery** — animations are calm and purposeful, matching the measured pace of rehabilitation

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort — no formal WCAG requirement. Focus on legibility, sufficient contrast, and touch target size for mobile-first use.
